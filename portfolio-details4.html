About this Project :-

This project aims to conduct an in-depth case study of Swiggy's operations and performance by employing SQL queries on a PostgreSQL database, managed through the pgAdmin4 administration tool. The database schema encompasses tables such as Food, Menu, Restaurants, Users, Order-Details, Orders, and Delivery-partners, each playing a crucial role in capturing the intricacies of Swiggy's ecosystem.

Database Design :-

Food Table:

  • Capturing details about individual food items offered on the platform, including names, types, and prices.

Menu Table:

  • Establishing the relationship between restaurants and the various food items they offer.

Restaurants Table:

  • Encompassing information about the partner restaurants, including names, locations, and contact details.

Users Table:

  • Storing details about Swiggy users, such as usernames, contact information, and preferences.

Order-Details Table:

  • Recording specifics about each item within an order, including quantities, prices, and applicable taxes.

Orders Table:

  • Tracking overarching details about individual orders, such as order numbers, user IDs, and timestamps.

Delivery-Partners Table:

  • Capturing information related to the delivery process, including delivery times, courier details, and delivery statuses.
